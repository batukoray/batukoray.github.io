Research Focus

The central interest is trustworthy behavior in language systems: what breaks, why it breaks, and how those failures can be measured well enough to matter. These interests span both the current SPADE work on membership inference and backdoor attacks, and independent lines of exploration beyond it.

01

Adversarial attacks on LLMs

Multi-turn jailbreaking strategies that exploit persuasion, context accumulation, and conversational framing rather than purely automated optimization.

02

NLP and transformer architectures

Language modeling, attention mechanisms, and the computational foundations of modern NLP, with a particular interest in how these systems behave under stress.

03

AI safety and alignment evaluation

Red-teaming methodologies, robustness evaluation, and the practical question of how safety fine-tuning can still be circumvented in deployed interfaces.

04

Mechanistic interpretability

Understanding internal representations and decision pathways, especially how models encode adversarial context and brittle cues across long interactions.
